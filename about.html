About the Project

I decided that I wanted to document a week’s worth of my trash in order to remind myself that what I throw away is a reflection of who I am. Every item is apart of a specific moment that can hold memories and meaning. The objects we throw away shows our routines, habits, and choices. By collecting my trash throughout the week, I saw these items as evidence of how I spend my time. Collecting and reflecting on my trash also made me more aware of how easily we throw things away without thinking about where they came from. Life encourages quick consumption and disposal, which can prevent us from seeing the bigger picture regarding our environmental impact. Artists like Vik Muniz have explored this by transforming garbage into huge artworks. In his Pictures of Garbage series, Muniz sculpted discarded materials from a landfill into really nice pieces that got turned into a photo which encouraged viewers to reconsider the value and meaning of waste. By turning trash into art, Muniz shows how something that is usually ignored can still hold significance. Similarly, artist sTo Len also looks into the hidden systems behind waste and pollution. His work is him collecting trash from natural environments and using it to make prints of environmental damage. Len shows the major impact that human consumption has on ecosystems. These artistic aspect of it shows that trash is not just something to discard, it is a material that can reveal a deeper impact about society and our relationship with the environment. Reflecting about what and how much we throw away can also reveal aspects of personal identity. Our trash can show our routines, interests, responsibilities, and lifestyle choices. It can even categorize us, since it shows our age, culture, or occupation. By looking into the waste we produce, we begin to recognize patterns in our behavior and consumption. What we throw away is not random, it reflects the choices we make every day. Overall, looking into our trash can become a reflection of personal identity and collective responsibility. By slowing down and paying attention to what we discard, we can better understand our habits and their impact. What just seems like meaningless waste can actually tell stories about who we are, how we live, and how we interact with the world around us. If you find this idea interesting, you might also want to explore artists like Sarah Newman, Vik Muniz, and sTo Len, who all examine how discarded materials can reveal deeper meanings about society, identity, and the environment.
